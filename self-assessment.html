Your Self Assessment year

Five dates worth knowing

The Self Assessment year follows the same rhythm every cycle. We track every one of these on your behalf — it still helps to know what’s coming.

  1. 01
    6 April

    New tax year begins

    The tax year for Self Assessment runs 6 April to 5 April.

  2. 02
    5 October

    Register with HMRC

    Deadline to register if you became self-employed that tax year.

  3. 03
    31 October

    Paper return due

    Deadline for paper Self Assessment submissions.

  4. 04
    31 January

    Online return & payment

    Online filing deadline and balancing payment due to HMRC.

  5. 05
    31 July

    Second payment on account

    Where applicable, your second advance payment falls due.

Common questions

Self Assessment, explained plainly

Still unsure about deadlines or records? Ask us directly — a clear answer beats another forum thread.

Ask a question

Anyone who is self-employed or a sole trader, a company director, a landlord, or who has untaxed income above certain thresholds generally needs to file a return with HMRC each year.

Paper returns are due by 31 October following the end of the tax year, and online returns by 31 January — the same date your balancing payment is due.

HMRC apply an initial fixed penalty, followed by further daily and percentage-based penalties the longer a return or payment stays outstanding, plus interest on late payments.

Sales invoices, expense receipts, bank statements and records of any other income, kept for at least five years after the filing deadline in case HMRC ask to see them.
