What We Do

Accounting built around how charities actually work

Charity accounts answer to trustees, donors and the Charity Commission all at once. Choose a category to see how we help.

Scrutiny

Independent Examinations

An independent, proportionate review of your accounts under Charity Commission Directions CC32 — the right level of scrutiny for charities below the audit threshold, carried out by an ACCA chartered examiner.

Reporting

Charities SORP Accounts

Accruals accounts prepared to the Charities SORP (FRS 102), including the new tiered Charities SORP 2026 framework — so your Statement of Financial Activities and trustees' report say exactly what they need to.

Reporting

Income & Expense Accounts

A clear, categorised breakdown of every pound in and out, giving trustees the financial picture they need to make decisions and satisfy funders without wading through raw transaction data.

Funds

Restricted Funds Analysis

Donor and grant conditions tracked to the pound, so restricted money is always spent — and reported — exactly as the donor intended, with a clean audit trail if the Commission or a funder ever asks.

Funds

Designated Funds Analysis

Trustee-earmarked funds set aside for a future purpose, tracked separately from general reserves, with the flexibility to release them back to unrestricted funds if plans change.

Funds

Unrestricted Funds Analysis

A clear view of the money you can deploy freely across your charitable purposes, so trustees can plan reserves and spending with confidence rather than guesswork.

Projects

Project Costing

Grant-funded projects tracked against budget and milestones in real time, so you can show funders precisely what their money achieved — not just what was spent.

Projects

Cost Centres

Costs split by department, service or site so you can see which parts of your work are efficient and which need attention — without changing how your team actually operates day to day.

Structure

Not-For-Profit Support

Financial reporting, compliance and governance advice built for mission-led organisations, so accountability to stakeholders never gets in the way of the work itself.

Structure

Charities Registration

Support through the Charity Commission registration process — governing documents, structure, purposes and public benefit — so you reach charitable status without the paperwork stalling your mission.

Structure

Companies Limited by Guarantee

Companies House and financial reporting handled together, so your CLG meets both company law and charity requirements without duplicated work or conflicting deadlines.

Questions Trustees Ask

Straight answers, no jargon

For years ending on or before 30 September 2026, you generally need an independent examination once income passes £25,000, and a full audit once income passes £1 million — or income passes £250,000 with assets over £3.26 million. For years ending after that date, those figures rise to £40,000 and £1.5 million (or £500,000 income with assets over £5 million). Your governing document or a funder can still require an audit regardless.

Yes, once income passes £250,000 (rising to £500,000 for years ending on or after 30 September 2026), the examiner must belong to a recognised accountancy body such as ACCA. Below that, they need relevant experience and a sound understanding of the Charities SORP.

It's the Statement of Recommended Practice that governs how charities preparing accruals accounts present their Statement of Financial Activities, balance sheet and trustees' annual report under FRS 102. The Charities SORP 2026 applies from accounting periods starting on or after 1 January 2026 and introduces a three-tier reporting structure based on income.

Restricted funds must go toward the purpose a donor or grant set, and trustees can't redirect them. Designated funds are unrestricted money trustees have earmarked for something specific, but can un-earmark later. Unrestricted funds carry no conditions at all and can support any of your charitable purposes.

Yes — non-company charities below the income threshold can choose the simpler receipts and payments basis. That threshold is £250,000 for years ending on or before 30 September 2026, rising to £500,000 after. Charitable companies must always prepare accruals accounts under the Companies Act.
