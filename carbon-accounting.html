What it is

Carbon accounting, explained the way we'd explain any other ledger

It's the discipline of measuring what your business emits, reporting it accurately, and using the numbers to manage risk and cost — not a one-off certificate.

As an accountancy firm, we treat your carbon footprint the way we treat any other set of figures: it has to be collected consistently, calculated correctly, and stand up to scrutiny. Our carbon accounting service covers the full cycle — gathering activity data on energy, fuel, transport and waste; converting it into a verified emissions inventory; and reporting it in the format your stakeholders expect.

Where it goes further than a compliance exercise is in the next step: using that inventory to find where reduction actually pays back, and building it into the financial reporting and decision-making you already do. Businesses that treat emissions data as part of ordinary financial discipline tend to make better, faster decisions about where to invest in efficiency.

Whether you're required to disclose under SECR, asked for figures by a customer's supply chain team, or simply want a credible baseline before setting a target, we build the report around what your business actually needs to show — and can defend.

How we work

From first data pull to a plan you can run

A structured, four-stage engagement — each stage builds directly on the last.

Stage 01

Baseline & data collection

We gather utility bills, fuel records, mileage and waste data across your sites and set the boundary for what's in scope.

Stage 02

Stage 03

Reporting & disclosure

We produce the report in the format you need — SECR, TCFD-aligned, or a customer's own supply chain questionnaire.

Stage 04

Reduction roadmap

We identify the highest-impact, most cost-effective reductions and build them into a plan with owners and dates.

Questions

Carbon accounting, the questions we're actually asked

Carbon accounting is the process of measuring, recording and reporting the greenhouse gases a business is responsible for, in the same disciplined way financial transactions are recorded. Businesses increasingly need it because customers, lenders, investors and regulators are asking for evidence of emissions performance, and because it is now a genuine driver of cost, risk and competitiveness.
Larger UK companies fall under Streamlined Energy and Carbon Reporting (SECR) and must disclose energy use and emissions in their annual accounts, while some listed and large private companies also have TCFD-aligned climate disclosure duties. Many smaller businesses are not yet legally required to report, but are asked to provide emissions data by customers, funders or supply chain partners. We can help you check exactly what applies to your business.
Scope 1 covers direct emissions from sources you own or control, such as company vehicles and on-site fuel combustion. Scope 2 covers emissions from purchased electricity, heat or steam. Scope 3 covers everything else in your value chain, including purchased goods and services, business travel, commuting and waste, and is often the largest share of a business's total footprint.
Activity data, such as litres of fuel, kilowatt-hours of electricity or miles travelled, is multiplied by an appropriate published emission factor and converted into a common unit, tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent (tCO2e), following the methodology set out in the Greenhouse Gas Protocol. The result is a structured inventory that can be reported, benchmarked and tracked year on year.
For most SMEs, a first baseline footprint can typically be produced within a few weeks once utility bills, fuel records and basic activity data are gathered, though timelines depend on how many sites and Scope 3 categories are in scope. We agree a realistic timeline with you at the start of the engagement.
